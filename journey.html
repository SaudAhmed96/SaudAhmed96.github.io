Programming Timeline

I took my first computer science class in 2013 without any knowledge of coding. I was exposed to binary and hexadecimal numbering schemes. We worked on a few projects, one of which I remember was a Tic-Tac-Toe game I created in C#, mostly copied from the internet. When we were not learning, the computer science class was playing Counter Strike. I recall that was when I first learned I sucked at shooter games since I always played Call of Duty in easy mode.

My teacher had a great passion for programming and at that time it rubbed off on me and I definitely had an interest to pursue it further but due to some personal issues I wasn't sure about it. I also liked designing things in CAD (Computer Aided Design). I ended up going to McMaster University for Mechanical Engineering & Management in the following year.

In my first year of university I had the opportunity to take a computer science course where we got to learn and use Python. I was able to naturally adapt and understand the concepts as I had some experience and found Python very freeing as it did not have some of the constraints that were required by C#.

While I was taking another course we were required to calculate possibilities for a gear train with a fixed number of gears and space constraints to house those gears. I recall I created a Python program to print out a number of lists that gave me the number of teeth required for each gear to meet the gear ratio within the size constraints. This was one of the first times I appreciated the ability to code and make my tasks easier.
